Our mission

Our mission is to promote the education of veterinary researchers, and to build a community of researchers who value and understand the relationship between people and animals. It has long been understood that the bond between humans and animals is a fundamental part of our daily lives, and our goal is to use our understanding of veterinary medicine to explore this relationship further to benefit all species.

Improving veterinary research

The NAVS is a community of DVM-PhD students whose sole purpose is the main purpose is the betterment of veterinary research.

Career exploration

There is no one "typical" career path for a DVM-PhD. Our graduates have diverse careers in academia, government, biotech, and other industries. Our organization builds connections between current students and alumni so that our members can learn about new career opportunities.

Creating community

NAVS continuously seeks to improve communications among dual degree programs by incorporating student representatives from each of the veterinary dual degree programs around the nation into the board of representatives, which is lead by and executive board of student representatives.

Learning together

Every year since 2017 students from veterinary dual degree programs around the country have attended an annual colloquium to share our research, build our community, and explore career opportunities for veterinary scientist.
